Dr. Marco Aurélio Franco

About me

Hello! My name is Marco Aurélio Franco, and I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the Atmospheric Physics Laboratory at the University of São Paulo in Brazil. My research interests focus on the connections between meteorology and ultrafine aerosols (particles with diameters < 50 nm) in Central Amazonia, with a particular emphasis on measurements conducted at the Amazon Tall Tower Observatory (ATTO) site.

I earned my Ph.D. in Physics from the Institute of Physics at the University of São Paulo, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Paulo Artaxo, on September 15th, 2021. During my Ph.D., I spent 13 months at the Max Planck Institute for Chemistry in Mainz, Germany, where I worked under the supervision of Dr. Christopher Pöhlker to integrate different databases and investigate the sub-50 nm particle aging process in Central Amazonia. If you're interested, you can access my Ph.D. thesis (in English) here: https://doi.org/10.11606/T.43.2021.tde-07102021-174257.

I also hold a Master's degree in Applied Physics, which I obtained at the São Carlos Institute of Physics of USP and Embrapa Instrumentation, under the supervision of Dr. Paulino Ribeiro Villas Boas. My bachelor's degree in Physics was obtained at the Department of Physics of the Federal University of São Carlos, where my undergraduate research was also supervised by Dr. Paulino Ribeiro Villas Boas. If you're interested, you can access my dissertation (in Portuguese) here: https://teses.usp.br/teses/disponiveis/76/76132/tde-13092017-135929/pt-br.php.

If you're interested in reading more about my main passions, including astronomy, astrophotography, music, math, and bikes, please visit my science blog: https://physmathscience.blogspot.com/. In the "Astrohobby" section, you'll find information on meteorological parameters, sky quality, the moon phase, and a sky map in real-time to help you plan your astronomical night. And remember: fight light pollution!

Research interests

I currently work on the interface of atmospheric mechanisms and physicochemical properties of atmospheric aerosols. I have interests in the mechanisms of secondary aerosol formation, aerosol aging processes, and radiative impacts of aerosols on the climate. In particular, I have been investigating different atmospheric processes that govern the dynamics of aerosols in the vertical profile of the lower Amazonian troposphere.
